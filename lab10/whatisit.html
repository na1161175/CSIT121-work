Touch Events

Touch events are the low-level APIs in web development and raw data from the touch hardware. JavaScript interprets that data and reacts to it on touch-enabled devices such as smartphones, touchscreen laptops, and tablets. They also allow the capturing of finger or stylus movement on screen and gestures such as swipes, taps, and multi-finger presses. These events provide a basis for dynamic, mobile-friendly applications that feel very natural to use on touchscreens.

The main touch events include:

  • touchstart - occurs when a touch point or a finger is placed on the touch surface.
  • touchend - when a touchpoint or a finger is removed from the touch surface.
  • touchmove - occurs when a touch point is dragged across the screen.
  • touchcancel â€“ when a touch point is interrupted, such as when multiple touch points are created.

Touch events consist of three key interfaces:

  • Touch interface - describing an individual touch point
  • TouchList interface - represents a list of touches
  • The TouchEvent interface - This represents an overall touch event with information about all active touches, changed touches, and modifier keys - such as shift or alt.
